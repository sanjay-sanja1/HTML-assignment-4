Essential Aspects of Web Accessibility:

  • Perceivable:
    • Provide text alternatives for non-text content.
    • Caption and provide transcripts for multimedia.
    • Ensure content is adaptable and distinguishable.
  • Operable:
    • Make all functionality available via a keyboard.
    • Provide sufficient time for users to read and interact with content.
    • Avoid content that could cause seizures or physical discomfort.
  • Understandable:
    • Create readable and understandable text.
    • Design consistent and predictable navigation and functionality.
    • Assist users in avoiding and correcting errors.
  • Robust:
    • Maximize compatibility with current and future technologies.

Why Accessibility is Essential:

  • Inclusivity:
    • Accessible websites ensure that everyone, including people with disabilities, can access and use information and services.
  • Legal Requirements:
    • Many countries have legal requirements and standards (e.g., ADA in the United States) mandating accessibility for public websites and applications.
  • Search Engine Optimization (SEO):
    • Accessible websites often have better SEO, as search engines prioritize content that is well-structured and usable.
  • Enhanced User Experience:
    • Accessibility features often benefit all users, not just those with disabilities. For example, subtitles in videos benefit users in noisy environments.
